# Optimal Korean Reference Characters for DM-Font

## Essential Reference Set (15 Characters)
# These characters provide excellent coverage of component variations while keeping the set minimal
'가' (U+AC00) - Basic structure with ㄱ initial + ㅏ vowel, no final consonant
'나' (U+B098) - Initial ㄴ with vowel ㅏ (shows horizontal-vertical combination)
'다' (U+B2E4) - Initial ㄷ with vowel ㅏ (shows box-like initial consonant)
'라' (U+B77C) - Shows ㄹ initial (horizontal consonant) + vowel ㅏ
'마' (U+B9C8) - Initial ㅁ with vowel ㅏ (shows square box initial)
'바' (U+BC14) - Initial ㅂ with vowel ㅏ (shows complex square initial)
'사' (U+C0AC) - Initial ㅅ with vowel ㅏ (shows diagonal strokes)
'자' (U+C790) - Initial ㅈ with vowel ㅏ (shows top-bottom stroke combination)
'차' (U+CC28) - Initial ㅊ with vowel ㅏ (shows complex top-heavy initial)
'카' (U+CE74) - Initial ㅋ with vowel ㅏ (shows extended vertical stroke)
'타' (U+D0C0) - Initial ㅌ with vowel ㅏ (shows horizontal-vertical balance)
'파' (U+D30C) - Initial ㅍ with vowel ㅏ (shows balance of empty and filled space)
'하' (U+D558) - Initial ㅎ with vowel ㅏ (shows curved strokes)
'밤' (U+BC24) - Demonstrates ㅂ initial + ㅏ vowel with vertical positioning + ㅁ final
'한' (U+D55C) - Shows ㅎ initial + ㅏ vowel + ㄴ final consonant

## Double Consonants (5 Characters)
'까' (U+AE00) - Double consonant ㄲ initial (shows stroke weight and tension)
'따' (U+B530) - Double consonant ㄸ initial (shows square double consonant)
'빠' (U+BE60) - Double consonant ㅃ initial (shows pressure point styling)
'싸' (U+C2F8) - Double consonant ㅆ initial (shows diagonal tension)

## Complex Vowels (6 Characters)

'귀' (U+ADC0) - Complex vowel ㅟ (combination of ㅜ+ㅣ showing vertical stacking)
'긔' (U+AE14) - Complex vowel ㅢ (combination of ㅡ+ㅣ showing horizontal-vertical)
'율' (U+C728) - Features ㅇ initial + complex vowel ㅠ + final ㄹ
'왕' (U+C655) - Shows ㅇ initial + complex vowel ㅘ + final ㅇ
